Why this matters

Knowing rotor parts by name helps you read diagrams quickly on tests and understand how control inputs reach the blades.

What Is a Rotor?

A rotor is the complete spinning assembly on top of a helicopter. It includes the mast (vertical shaft), the hub (center connection), and the blades (long airfoils).

The engine turns the mast, the mast turns the hub, and the hub spins the blades. As the blades spin, they generate lift.

Main Rotor vs Tail Rotor

Main Rotor

  • Creates most of the lift
  • Sits on top of the aircraft
  • Large diameter
  • Connected to engine via transmission

Tail Rotor

  • Counters main rotor torque
  • Mounted on the tail
  • Smaller diameter
  • Also controls yaw direction

Olympiad Edge

Distinguish rotor system parts from aircraft body parts. The mast, hub, and blades are part of the rotor system. The fuselage, tail boom, and landing gear are airframe parts. Tests may mix them to check your precision.

Key Vocabulary

Mast
The vertical shaft supporting the rotor.
Hub
The central part connecting blades to the mast.
Main rotor
The primary spinning assembly that creates lift.
Tail rotor
Small rotor that counters torque and controls yaw.
