Autorotation

A safety descent when the engine goes quiet.

Why this matters

Autorotation is an advanced concept that distinguishes top competitors. It shows deep understanding of how rotors interact with air.

What Autorotation Is

Autorotation is an unpowered descent. If the engine fails, the pilot enters autorotation. Air flowing upward through the rotor keeps the blades spinning. This spinning creates enough lift to control the descent and land safely.

Why It Works

Normally, engine power spins the rotor and air moves downward. In autorotation, the helicopter descends, so air moves upward through the rotor disk. This upward airflow pushes the blades and keeps them turning.

Near the ground, the pilot pulls the collective to increase blade pitch. The stored rotor energy creates a final burst of lift for a soft landing.

Olympiad Edge

This concept can distinguish advanced learners. Key points: in autorotation, airflow reverses direction (upward through rotor). The rotor is not powered by the engine. The pilot uses collective at the bottom to flare and soften landing.
