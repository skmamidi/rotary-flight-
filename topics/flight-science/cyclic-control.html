Why this matters

The cyclic is how pilots steer helicopters. Understanding it separates rotary flight from fixed-wing control systems.

What Cyclic Is

The cyclic control changes the pitch of each rotor blade at a specific point in its rotation. This tilts the entire rotor disk, directing thrust and making the helicopter move.

  • Push cyclic forward → rotor tilts forward → helicopter moves forward
  • Push cyclic back → rotor tilts back → helicopter moves backward
  • Push cyclic left → rotor tilts left → helicopter moves left
  • Push cyclic right → rotor tilts right → helicopter moves right

Olympiad Edge

Cyclic tilts the rotor disk for direction. Collective changes all blade angles for lift. Tests love to swap these definitions. Memorize: Cyclic = Circle/Direction; Collective = Climb/All blades.

Key Vocabulary

Cyclic
Control that tilts the rotor disk for directional flight.
Rotor disk
The imaginary circle traced by spinning blades.
Pitch
The angle of a blade relative to the oncoming air.
