Collective Control

Change all blade angles at once to climb or descend.

Why this matters

Collective is how pilots control altitude. It is one of the most important controls and is frequently tested against cyclic.

What Collective Is

The collective control raises or lowers the pitch of all rotor blades at the same time by the same amount. More pitch = more lift = climb. Less pitch = less lift = descent.

How It Works

  • Pull up collective → blade pitch increases → more lift → helicopter climbs
  • Lower collective → blade pitch decreases → less lift → helicopter descends
  • Hold steady → pitch stays constant → lift equals weight → level flight or hover

Olympiad Edge

Very important comparison: Collective changes all blades equally (total lift). Cyclic changes blades selectively (direction). If a test asks which control is used to climb, the answer is collective.
