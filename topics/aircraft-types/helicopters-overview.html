Why this matters

Helicopters are the most familiar rotary-wing aircraft. Understanding their basic layout and capabilities is essential before comparing them to autogyros and tiltrotors.

What Is a Helicopter?

A helicopter is a rotary-wing aircraft with a powered main rotor. The engine spins the rotor blades, creating lift. Because the rotor is powered, the helicopter can hover, take off vertically, and fly in any direction.

Olympiad Edge

Helicopters are defined by their powered rotor and hover capability. When comparing aircraft on tests, always check: Is the lift rotor powered? Can it hover? These two questions separate helicopters from autogyros instantly.

Key Vocabulary

Helicopter
Aircraft with powered rotor that can hover and take off vertically.
Powered rotor
A rotor driven directly by the engine to create lift.
VTOL
