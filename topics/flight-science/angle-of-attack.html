The angle that makes or breaks lift.

Why this matters

Angle of attack controls how much lift a blade generates. Too high, and the blade stalls.

What Angle of Attack Means

The angle of attack is the angle between the blade and the oncoming air. A small angle gives gentle lift. A larger angle gives more lift but also more drag.

Stall Warning

If the angle becomes too high, airflow separates from the blade and lift drops suddenly. This is called a stall.
