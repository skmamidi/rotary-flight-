Why this matters

Hovering is one of the most impressive and useful things a helicopter can do. It is also one of the most difficult skills for a pilot to master.

What Hovering Means

Hovering means remaining nearly stationary in the air. The helicopter stays over one spot on the ground without moving forward, backward, or sideways.

To hover, the pilot must balance lift against weight and make tiny control corrections to fight wind and drift.

Balance of Lift and Weight

In a hover, lift must equal weight exactly. If lift is too low, the helicopter sinks. If lift is too high, it climbs. The pilot uses the collective control to adjust overall lift.

Olympiad Edge

Hovering is controlled balance, not simply "stopping the aircraft." The pilot must make constant, tiny corrections. Tests may ask why hovering is difficult—answer with balance and active control, not just engine power.

Key Vocabulary

Hover

Collective
