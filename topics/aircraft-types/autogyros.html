Autogyros

Rotorcraft that look like helicopters but fly differently.

Advanced ⏱ 8 min ○ Not completed
⚠️
Critical Distinction

Autogyros are not helicopters. This is one of the most commonly missed questions on rotary flight tests. Read carefully.

🎯
Why this matters

Science Olympiad tests frequently ask students to distinguish autogyros from helicopters. Knowing the difference in rotor power and hover ability earns easy points.

What Is an Autogyro?

An autogyro (also called a gyrocopter) has a rotor like a helicopter, but the rotor is not powered by the engine for lift. Instead, the rotor spins because air passes through it as the aircraft moves forward.

Forward thrust comes from a separate propeller, similar to an airplane. The unpowered rotor simply autorotates, creating lift from the airflow.

Olympiad Edge

This is a high-frequency trap. Remember:

  • Autogyro rotor = unpowered (autorotates from airflow)
  • Helicopter rotor = powered by engine
  • Autogyro cannot hover
  • Autogyro uses a propeller for thrust

If a test says "an autogyro is a type of helicopter," that statement is false.

Key Vocabulary

Autogyro
Aircraft with unpowered rotor and propeller for thrust.
Autorotation
Rotor spinning from airflow, not engine power.
Propeller
Spinning blade that creates forward thrust.
