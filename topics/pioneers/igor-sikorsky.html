Why this matters

Igor Sikorsky is the most famous name in helicopter history. Tests frequently mention him and the VS-300.

Who Sikorsky Was

Igor Sikorsky (1889–1972) was a Russian-American aviation pioneer. He designed and flew the VS-300 in 1939, the first successful single-main-rotor helicopter in the United States.

Key Contributions

  • Developed the single-main-rotor with tail rotor configuration
  • Proved that practical, controllable helicopter flight was possible
  • Founded Sikorsky Aircraft Corporation
  • Designed helicopters used in military, rescue, and civilian roles

Timeline Highlights

1889 — Born in Kiev, Russian Empire
1939 — First flight of the VS-300
1942 — R-4 becomes first mass-produced helicopter
1972 — Passes away in the United States
