Torque

Newton's Third Law in action: spin the rotor, and the body wants to spin back.

Why this matters

Torque is one of the most tested concepts in rotary flight competitions. If you understand action-reaction, you understand why helicopters need tail rotors or coaxial designs.

What Torque Is

Torque is a twisting force. When a helicopter's engine spins the main rotor in one direction, the helicopter body tends to spin in the opposite direction. This is Newton's Third Law: for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

The Action-Reaction Pair

↻

Action

Engine spins rotor clockwise (as seen from above).

↺

Reaction

Fuselage tries to spin counter-clockwise.

🏆
Olympiad Edge

One of the highest-value competition concepts. Remember: torque is not drag. Torque is a twisting reaction from the rotor. Drag is air resistance. Tests often pair them as answer choices to trap students.

Key Vocabulary
