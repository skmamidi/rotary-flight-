Why this matters

The VS-300 is a milestone in aviation history. Tests often reference it as the first successful practical helicopter.

What the VS-300 Was

The Vought-Sikorsky VS-300 was the first successful single-main-rotor helicopter in the United States. It first flew on September 14, 1939.

It used a single main rotor for lift and a small tail rotor to counter torque. This layout became the standard for most helicopters worldwide.

Key Design Features

  • Single three-bladed main rotor
  • Tail rotor for anti-torque and yaw control
  • Open-framework fuselage
  • Powered by a 75-horsepower engine

Legacy

The VS-300 proved that helicopters could be practical, not just experimental. It led directly to the R-4, the first mass-produced helicopter, used in World War II.

🏆
Olympiad Edge

Connect the VS-300 to practical helicopter development. It was not the first machine to ever leave the ground, but it was the first successful, controllable, practical helicopter configuration.
